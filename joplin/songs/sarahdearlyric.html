Sarah Dear, Lyrics

Written by Henry Jackson, 1905.

(Piano Intro.)
Just a-bout the time that I fall a-sleep, I'm thinking of my Sa-rah dear;
And as I a-wake from my slum-ber deep, I'm still think-ing of Sa-rah dear;
When e'er I get a glimpse of you dear girl, my heart is filled with glee;
When you are with me-- it's pleasure you see,-- with you I long to be.

(Chorus)
O! you are my Sa-rah dear, the sun-light of my soul;---
You have won my heart and hand All of my love you stole;
You are sweet-ness to my lips your ten-der voice I love to hear,-
And when I am near,- you've noth-ing to fear,- be-lieve me Sa-rah dear.

You're the sweet-est girl that I could find, you my Sa-rah dear;
And you caused a thought to come to my mind, you my Sa-rah dear;
I've a ques-tion that I would ask of you, you can make me hap-py through life;
And when we're a-lone the truth will be known, I'll ask you to be my wife.

(Repeat Chorus)
