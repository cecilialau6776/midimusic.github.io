Recognize Music

Do You Recognize Any Of This Music?

Please listen to the music below and see if you can recognize (and can put a name to) any of these tunes, they are all waltzes. Also listen to see if you agree with the naming of those which have been identified. As of October 2014 I have updated the recordings. They are now played on a fully instrumented Wurlitzer 125 using my latest automated e-roll player.

Use the w125 and w150 catalogues to find more candidate tunes from the 1923 era.

If you can name any of the tunes or disagree with any of the identified tunes please contact me at the email address in "About MidiMusic". Your reward will be to have your name associated with the piece you recognize.

I will keep this page updated with any progress. I will also continue the search myself.

1.0 Unknown Wurlitzer Roll w93

This is a genuine Wurlitzer 125 roll which we now know is uncatalogued and there is no matching w150 roll. It is likely to be roll 3092 as this is the only 1923 or later uncatalogued roll until roll 3136 in 1928. The next steps are to identify each tune. This is a genuine Wurlitzer 125 roll which was most likely issued around 1923. All of the unknown tunes are waltzes but bear in mind that the lost tunes may not be waltzes. Identifying just one tune should be enough to identify the roll, then the other tunes can be found from the w125 catalogue.

It could be an uncatalogued roll but only if it is prior to 1917 or later than 1928, unless it is roll 3092, now unlikely; see above.

2.1 Example candidate w125 Rolls for w82

Use the catalogue below to find more.
Wurlitzer 125 roll catalogue (searchable). All known Wurlitzer 125 Long Rolls from 1913 to 1964.

I have a strong suspicion that it is roll 3081 listed below (=w150 roll 13096), but I have so far found only tunes -05, -07 and -10 which are available above for comparison. I find comparison is very difficult and I cannot be sure one way or the other but there are strong similarities for all three tunes.

I have the score for -03 and again, it "sort of" matches.

I have the score for -05 and can say for certain that Wurlitzer are not following it whereas the comparison My Buddy follows it exactly.

I have the score for -07 and can say for certain that Wurlitzer are not following it. The comparison does not follow it either but is much closer than Wurlitzer.

I have the score for -10 and can say for certain that Wurlitzer are not following it. The comparison follows it exactly but carries on with a repeat which is not scored.

I do not have either roll 3081 or roll 13096 available to me so I cannot check directly.

Where does this leave things? Just because Wurlitzer did not follow the score does not necessarily mean that there is no match. Did they ever follow the score? I have my doubts, they always embellished their music with notes not in the score but did they make up their own versions of similar sounding tunes, possibly because some of their musical arrangers were incompetent or perhaps purposely to avoid tax and copyright payments?

At one time, Wurlitzer's music roll arrangers were paid a nickel a foot for their arrangements, a pay scale that didn't contribute to spending any more time on arranging than absolutely necessary.

Research with other rolls which have already been identified is needed to clarify this.
